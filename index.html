  • Overview
    The goal of this class is to teach students the necessary concepts and programming skills to work on a self-driving car. To achieve this, students will be given lectures/labs on a variety of different content in order to familiarize themselves with some of the challenges embedded in the self-driving problem. The scope of content will include Deep Learning/Computer Vision, Sensor Fusion, and Control Systems. In addition, students will be exposed to a lot of the specific challenges that self-driving cars face, and will be challenged to find solutions to these issues.

Course Policies

  • Labs
    • All lab reports must be submitted by the deadline specified in the course schedule. No late submission is accepted unless instructor or TAs are notified at least 2 days ahead of the deadline.
    • Unless specified otherwise, all lab works must be done individually. Students will be reported to SJA for suspicion of cheating or unauthorized colaborations. That said, high-level discussions are acceptable, and students are encouraged to help each other during lab (clarifying lab's objective, discussing high-level concepts, approaches, ... ). Consult instructor or TAs if it is unclear an activity counted as unauthorized colaborations.
    • Plagiarism in lab reports and source codes is strictly forbidden. This applied to material found online. Viewing source codes that can aid finishing the lab from the Internet or other classmates can influenced how students approach solving the lab's problem. So, it is highly recommended not to search up the solution to the lab. There are mechanisms in place that check for plagiarism in students' submitted source codes and lab reports. All incidents of suspected plagiarism will be reported to SJA.
    • Each student is provided with a lab key. It is the student's responsibility to return the key at the end of the courses. Attempts to replicate the key will be reported.
    • Students may only use class hardware and machines strictly for class-related purposes. Any other activities are forbidden.
  • Grading Errors and Post-submission Discussion
    The majority of the students' grade comes from their lab works and reports. The course provides opportunities for students to do well by giving students a chance to discuss their grades. It is possible for students to request regrades, if one of the following conditions applied:
    • Student think there is a grading error. Lab coding solutions will be released for each lab after submission deadline.
    • Student think they can demonstrate their understanding of the material better after receiving the grade. This applied mainly to the lab report. The course objective is to help students prepared for the AI industry. Students should view their grades as constructive feedbacks designed to help students find areas which they need to improve on. After recieving the grade, and through addressing areas in need of improvement, students can setup a demonstration session with TAs to prove that they have understood the given material. If TAs find the demonstration satisfactory, the student's grade will be raised to theoretically 100% of the assignment. Demonstration session can must be scheduled outside of TAs office hours, either in person or online through Slack conference.
